Rules

Participants will debate in teams of three, each speaker will be given 5mins to deliver there speech, in their first two minutes they are protected, when they reach their 3rd,4th,and 5th minutes they can be given points of information , inquiry and order. Before the debate begins the two teams will be given a motion or resolution on which they will prepare for their first 10minutes. then the chair will procede with the order of the agenda. You will not be given access to any external data source, neither a phone, tablet nor PC. Debates will be judged basing on the following factors:

  • Eloquence [40 marks]
  • Outstanding Skills [40 marks]
  • Cooperativeness [40 marks]
  • Number of points laid in relation to those rebutted [40 marks]
  • Time keeping [40 marks]

NB: The chair persons are only allowed to give a maximum of 200 points for the winning group and 40 points for each categorized skill of the group .

The group with the most marks wins the debate and proceeds to the next level. The first debates will be elimination phases and the eliminated team should not be discouraged because they still have a chance of getting an award of either having the best (outstanding debaters.) , Academician etc.

The group that reach final will be awarded, but a better award goes to the Grand finale winner
